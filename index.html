The Profit Function

π(p, s, a, i) = p·D(p,s,a) − C(i, D)

D = demand (depends on price, shelf space, ads). C = cost (depends on inventory & demand). This is a multivariable function — output depends on multiple inputs simultaneously.

Why Not Single-Variable?

Single-variable calculus asks: "how does profit change with price alone?" But real decisions couple together. You need partial derivatives — ∂π/∂p, ∂π/∂s, ∂π/∂a, ∂π/∂i — one for each lever.

Chain Rule Equation

dπ/dp = (dπ/dC) · (dC/dW) · (dW/dI) · (dI/dD) · (dD/dp)

Each domino arrow is a derivative. The chain rule multiplies them all to find the total effect of a price change on profit — this is exactly how neural networks compute backpropagation.

Key Insight: A small price increase can destroy profit when downstream consequences amplify. Leo lost money despite charging more!
